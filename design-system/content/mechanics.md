# Mechanics

Grammar and house style decisions most likely to affect generated UI copy.

---

## Capitalization

- **Sentence case everywhere**: buttons, labels, headings, nav items, error messages, table headers, empty states.
- **Title case** only for proper nouns, brand names, and official document titles (Terms of Use, Privacy Policy).
- Never use ALL CAPS for emphasis — use typography weight instead.

---

## Contractions

Allowed and preferred. They keep the voice natural: "You're all set", "We can't find that page", "We'll email you shortly."

Avoid contractions in legal or high-stakes instructions where ambiguity could arise.

---

## Numbers

| Rule | Example |
|------|---------|
| Numerals for 10 and above | "10 results", "25,000 sellers" |
| Spell out one through nine in running prose | "three options", "two business days" |
| Prefer numerals in dense UI (tables, labels, buttons) | "2 items", "4 GB" |
| Thousands separator: comma | 1,000 / 25,000 |
| Percentages: no space before % | 20% |
| Ranges: en dash, no spaces | 2–4 days |
| Units: space between number and unit | 64 GB, 5 h, 20 min |
| Units do not pluralize | 1 GB, 2 GB |

---

## Punctuation

**Labels, buttons, headings, nav items:**
- No ending punctuation.
- No ellipsis unless an action genuinely opens a follow-up dialog requiring more input.
- No ampersand in body copy; use "and". Ampersands are allowed in tight headline constraints.
- Avoid exclamation points; never more than one per surface.

**Body copy, helper text, error messages, toasts:**
- Use standard sentence punctuation.
- One sentence per message where possible.
- Oxford comma: use it.

---

## Standardized terms

| Term | Correct form | Notes |
|------|-------------|-------|
| Brand name | Back Market | Two words, both capitalized. Never "BackMarket" or "back market". |
| Log in (verb) | log in | "Log in to your account" |
| Login (noun/adj) | login | "Enter your login details" |
| Log out (verb) | log out | "Log out of Back Market" |
| Sign up (verb) | sign up | "Sign up for free" |
| Sign-up (noun/adj) | sign-up | "Complete the sign-up form" |
| Authentication | Log in / Log out | Use "log in" consistently across product UI; avoid mixing with "sign in" |
| Wi-Fi | Wi-Fi | Hyphenated |
| Email | email | Lowercase, one word |
| Website | website | One word |
| BuyBack | BuyBack | Our program name in product contexts; "trade-in" only for external comprehension |
| CO₂ | CO₂ | Subscript where supported; "CO2" if not |
| Business days | business days | Never "working days" |
| US | US | No periods as an adjective; "the United States" as a noun |
| E-commerce | e-commerce | Hyphenated |

---

## Words to avoid

| Avoid | Use instead |
|-------|------------|
| "Click here" | Specific action verb + context |
| "Submit" (standalone) | Specific outcome: "Place order", "Save changes" |
| "Proceed" | "Continue" or "Continue to [step]" |
| "Please note that…" | State the fact directly |
| Ampersand (&) in body copy | "and" |
| ALL CAPS for emphasis | Bold or typographic weight |
| Exclamation points (except sparingly) | — |
| Internal jargon / acronyms | Plain terms users recognize |
| Competitor references | — |
| "Working days" | "Business days" |
